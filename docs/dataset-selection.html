Starting Hypotheses

  • Students from higher socioeconomic backgrounds tend to score higher than students from lower socioeconomic backgrounds.
  • Students whose parents have higher levels of education are more likely to perform well at the ICFES.
  • There is a correlation between the number of hours a student spends on labor per week and their ICFES scores.
  • Students in bilingual schools may have better reading comprehension skills compared to students in non-bilingual schools due to exposure to a diverse linguistic environment.
  • Access to a nutritious diet translates into better ICFES scores.
